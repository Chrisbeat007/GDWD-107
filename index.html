The Brown Bear

About Brown bears

The Brown bear (Ursus Arctos) is native to parts of northern Eurasia and North America. Its convsercation status is currently least concern.

There are many subspecies within within the brown bear species, inlcuding the atlas bear and the Himalayan brown bear.

Species

  • Collarus
  • Horribilis
  • Nelsoni (Extinct)

Features

brown bears are not always completly brown. Some can be reddish or yellowish. They have very large, curved claws and huge paws. Male brown bears are oftne 30% larger that female brown bears. They can range from 5 feet to 9 feet from head to toe.
